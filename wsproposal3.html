BIS 23

Requirements Document

A proposal for a youth-centered cultural travel agency website — focusing on front-end design, user experience, and interactive elements.

1. Project Description

What problem, business, or idea are you addressing?

This project focuses on creating a youth-centered travel agency website that highlights cultural festivals and meaningful travel experiences around the world. Many young adults are interested in attending international festivals and exploring new cultures, but travel websites often feel overwhelming, cluttered, or difficult to navigate.

This website aims to provide a clean, visually engaging, and user-friendly platform where young travelers can easily explore destinations, view travel packages, learn about cultural events, and request personalized itineraries.

What inspired this idea?

This idea was inspired by a first trip to another continent — specifically South Korea — which became one of the most meaningful experiences and motivated continued exploration of different cultures and festivals around the world.

Planning that first international trip required a lot of research, organizing, and comparing options, which felt overwhelming at times. That experience inspired designing a travel agency platform that feels clear, organized, and easy to use — especially for young adults planning their first big international adventure.

2. Purpose & Application

Who is the target audience?

Young adults between the ages of 18 and 30 who are interested in cultural exploration, festivals, and international travel — including college students and young professionals looking for adventurous and meaningful experiences.

What problem does your site help solve?

Many travel websites feel overwhelming, crowded with information, or difficult to navigate. Young travelers often struggle to find clear information about festivals, destinations, and trip planning without feeling confused.

This website helps solve that by providing a clean layout, organized navigation, and interactive features that make exploring travel options simple and engaging.

What real-world application does it have?

In a real-world setting, this website functions as a front-facing travel agency platform where users can explore destinations, view travel packages, learn about cultural festivals, and submit requests for booking or personalized itineraries. It emphasizes responsive design, smooth navigation, and interactive elements that improve the overall user experience.

3. Website Structure (Site Model)

Inside each page, the full description will appear. For example:
Packages → "Travel Packages & Experiences"
Itinerary → "Request a Custom Itinerary"
Book → "Submit a Booking Request"

The navigation bar will be simple for now. If the project develops further, dropdown features may be added later in the semester.

4. Page Descriptions

Home

Introduces the travel agency and its mission with a visually engaging layout, strong imagery from cultural festivals, featured trips, and call-to-action buttons guiding users to explore packages or festivals.

Packages

Displays available travel experiences in an organized layout. Users can browse trips and click on individual packages to view more details. May include hover effects, image galleries, or filtering options. Each package includes a comment section.

Festivals

Highlights important cultural events around the world using images, cards, or sections. Users can click on a festival to learn more or navigate to related travel packages.

Itinerary

Allows users to request a personalized travel plan through a structured form with client-side validation to ensure required fields are completed before submitting.

Contact

A contact form with clear input fields and a simple design to encourage communication with the agency.

5. Design Concept

How should the site look and feel?

The website should feel exciting, inspiring, and easy to use — like a real travel agency, but also youthful and adventurous, with users feeling ready to book a trip after browsing.

It will include images of cultural festivals, important landmarks, and meaningful travel moments. Colors will use blue and orange — blue for trust and calmness, orange to add energy and excitement.

The layout will be clean and simple, with clear navigation and a design that works well on both desktop and mobile. The main focus is to keep it modern, organized, and visually engaging.

6. Results & Success

How will you know if your website is working?

The website will be successful if users can move through the pages easily and understand how everything works without confusion. If visitors can explore packages, learn about festivals, and submit forms without issues, then the site is doing its job.

Success will also be shown if users interact with the platform — clicking on packages, navigating between pages smoothly, and completing forms correctly. If the layout feels clear, responsive, and easy to use on both desktop and mobile, the website is functioning properly.
