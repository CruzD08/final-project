BIS 31

Requirements Document

A proposal for a youth-focused cultural travel agency website, outlining the project concept, target audience, structure, design, and success criteria.

1. Project Description

What problem, business, or idea are you addressing?

This project addresses the challenges young travelers face when planning cultural and international trips. Many young adults want to attend global festivals, explore new countries, and experience meaningful cultural events, but the process of planning international travel can feel overwhelming.

This website represents a youth-focused travel agency that specializes in cultural festivals and immersive experiences. The agency simplifies the travel planning process by offering curated travel packages, human-designed custom itineraries, and organized festival-based trips. Instead of generic tourism, the business focuses on meaningful cultural adventures designed specifically for young travelers seeking growth, connection, and unforgettable experiences.

What inspired this idea?

This idea was inspired by a first trip to another continent — specifically South Korea — which became one of the most meaningful and transformative experiences. It opened a new perspective, made the world feel bigger, and inspired continued travel and cultural exploration.

However, planning that first international trip was complicated. Organizing itineraries, reservations, activities, and transportation for a 15+ day trip required a lot of research and effort. That experience inspired the creation of a travel agency that makes international travel more accessible and less overwhelming for young adults.

2. Purpose & Application

Who is the target audience?

Young adults between the ages of 18 and 30 who are interested in cultural exploration, international festivals, and meaningful travel experiences. This includes college students, young professionals, and adventurous travelers — especially those planning their first international trip.

What problem does your site help solve?

The website helps solve the problem of complicated and overwhelming travel planning. Many young travelers struggle with:

  • Creating structured itineraries
  • Finding authentic cultural events
  • Booking accommodations and activities
  • Organizing long international trips
  • Identifying meaningful festival experiences

This agency simplifies the process by offering curated travel packages, festival-based experiences, and personalized itinerary services.

What real-world application does it have?

Users will be able to browse travel packages, explore international festivals, submit booking requests, and request personalized itineraries based on their travel preferences. Each travel package will include a comment section where users can share interest, ask questions, or connect with others considering the same experience.

3. Website Structure (Site Model)

Inside each page, the full description will appear. For example:
Packages → "Travel Packages & Experiences"
Itinerary → "Request a Custom Itinerary"
Book → "Submit a Booking Request"

The navigation bar will be simple without dropdown menus for now. If the project expands, dropdown features may be added later in the semester.

4. Page Descriptions

Home

Introduces the travel agency and its mission. Highlights the focus on cultural festivals and meaningful experiences, with featured trips and navigation to other sections.

Packages

Displays available travel experiences and destinations. Users can view trip descriptions, duration, pricing, and what is included. Each package includes a comment section.

Festivals

Highlights important cultural events around the world — lantern festivals in Thailand, cherry blossom season in Japan, New Year in Brazil, Summer Concerts, and other major celebrations.

Itinerary

Allows users to request a personalized travel plan by submitting details such as destination, number of days, travel style, and preferences.

Book

Allows users to submit a booking request for a selected travel package, providing basic information such as name, email, travel date, and number of travelers.

Contact

Allows users to send general inquiries or questions to the agency through a contact form.

5. Design Concept

How should the site look and feel?

The website should feel modern, inviting, and inspiring — like a real travel agency that motivates users to start planning their next adventure. The goal is for visitors to feel excited and confident, almost ready to book their trip.

The site will include images of cultural festivals, iconic landmarks, and meaningful travel experiences. The color palette will use blue and orange — blue to represent trust, calmness, and reliability, while orange brings energy and excitement. The final color palette and layout may evolve as the project develops.

6. Results & Success

How will you know if your website is working?

The website will be successful if users can move through the pages easily and understand how to use it without confusion. Success is shown when users can browse travel packages, explore festival information, submit booking requests, request itineraries, and leave comments on specific trips.

It will also be considered successful if all user submissions — booking forms, itinerary requests, comments, and contact messages — are properly collected and organized within the system.
